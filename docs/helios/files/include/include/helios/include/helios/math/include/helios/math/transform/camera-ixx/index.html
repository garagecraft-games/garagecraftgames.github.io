13 * The method creates the camera coordinate system based on the
14 * parameters eye, i.e. the vantage point in world space, the camera's
15 * up direction and center, representing the point of interest.
16 * Note that the returned matrix is essentially a composition of
17 * a change of coordinates matrix P(C <- W) and a translation
18 * to the origin, i.e. the camera is sitting at the origin (0, 0, 0)
19 * looking down the negative z-axis, i.e. conceptually
20 *
21 * P(W<-C) * T(-eye)
22 *
23 * The 4x4 matrix can then be used for computing the perspective projection
24 * for creating the clip space

26 * clip = P * V * W * M

28 * @param eye
29 * @param center
